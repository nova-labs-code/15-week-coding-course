Week 20: Launch, Maintenance & Growth – Extended

This final week focuses on **launching professionally, maintaining your site, and planning growth** for long-term success. Each day includes exercises, tips, and best practices.

Day 1: Final Site Check

Learn: Review every page, link, form, and interactive element to ensure everything works as intended.

Do: Open each page in multiple browsers and devices, test forms, buttons, images, and media. Look for broken links, missing content, or errors.

Mini Exercise: Make a checklist of all pages and interactive elements, marking any issues found and fixing them.
Tip: Walk through your site as a first-time visitor would. Try to perform common tasks like signing up, contacting you, or finding content.
Day 2: Backups & Security

Learn: Protect your site with SSL, secure hosting, regular backups, and strong passwords.

Do: Enable SSL, schedule automatic backups, use strong passwords, and check firewall or security plugins if applicable.

Mini Exercise: Test restoring a backup in a safe environment to ensure your recovery process works.
Tip: Backups should be stored off-site or in the cloud for extra safety. Regularly test recovery.
Day 3: Hosting & Domain

Learn: Ensure your hosting provider is reliable and your domain is configured properly.

Do: Check server uptime, loading speed, domain DNS settings, and SSL configuration. Verify your website is publicly accessible.

Mini Exercise: Use tools like Pingdom or GTmetrix to check server response and page load speed.
Tip: Good hosting improves speed, uptime, and user trust. Ensure DNS is correct to avoid downtime.
Day 4: Launch Checklist

Learn: Confirm SEO setup, analytics, social links, email integrations, and announcement plans are complete before going live.

Do: Follow a detailed launch checklist covering meta tags, sitemap, robots.txt, analytics, social media links, and content review.

Mini Exercise: Tick off each item and verify that all features work correctly on desktop and mobile.
Tip: A thorough pre-launch checklist prevents post-launch issues and ensures a smooth start for visitors.
Day 5: Post-Launch Monitoring

Learn: Track traffic, errors, and user behavior immediately after launch to catch any issues.

Do: Review analytics daily, fix broken links, review feedback forms, and monitor server performance.

Mini Exercise: Log 3–5 issues found in the first 48 hours and implement fixes.
Tip: Rapid response builds user trust and prevents small issues from becoming bigger problems.
Day 6: Continuous Improvement

Learn: Use data and feedback to improve site content, usability, and engagement over time.

Do: Pick a page or feature and implement one improvement based on analytics or user suggestions. Track the impact.

Mini Exercise: Make a small change (layout, button text, image, or content) and compare engagement before and after.
Tip: Continuous iteration keeps your site relevant, faster, and more enjoyable for visitors.
Day 7: Plan Next Steps & Growth

Learn: Create a roadmap for content updates, promotion strategies, and audience growth.

Do: Schedule blog posts, social media campaigns, or email newsletters. Consider partnerships or monetization opportunities.

Mini Exercise: Draft a 3–6 month plan with goals, deadlines, and KPIs to track growth.
Tip: Regular planning ensures your website continues to attract visitors and grow sustainably.
