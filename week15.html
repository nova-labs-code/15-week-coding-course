Week 15: Website Strategy & Purpose – Extended

This week focuses on **planning your website’s purpose, audience, content, and structure**. This extended version includes additional exercises and deeper insights to help you create a solid strategy.

Day 1: Define Your Purpose

Learn: Every website needs a clear purpose. Ask yourself:

  • What problem does my website solve?
  • Who benefits from it?
  • Why is my site different from others?

Do: Write 1–3 sentences summarizing your site’s main goal. Be specific. Avoid vague statements like "I want a website about tech." Instead, try: "I want to create a website that teaches teens basic coding in interactive lessons."

Mini Exercise: Write your purpose and list 3 actions your website should enable users to do.
Tip: Your purpose will guide every design and content decision. Keep it visible while planning.
Day 2: Audience Research

Learn: Knowing your audience helps tailor content and design. Consider:

  • Age and location
  • Interests and hobbies
  • Comfort level with technology
  • Goals and expectations

Do: Create 2–3 target audience profiles. Include name, age range, goals, and what they might want from your website.

Mini Exercise: Write a few bullet points describing each audience type. Example:
“Teen coders, 13–17 years old, want interactive lessons and easy examples.”
Tip: Profiles will influence layout, color choices, font size, and even tone of writing.
Day 3: Planning Site Structure

Learn: Organize content logically. Common page hierarchy:

  • Home: Overview
  • About: Who you are
  • Content pages: Lessons, products, or services
  • Contact: Form or info
  • Blog or Updates (optional)

Do: Draw a sitemap showing main pages and subpages. Include arrows to show navigation flow.

Mini Exercise: Try using a tool like Figma, Miro, or pen & paper. Highlight which pages are most important to your audience.
Tip: Keep navigation simple. Users should reach important content in 2–3 clicks.
Day 4: Branding Basics

Learn: Branding ensures users recognize your website. Key elements:

  • Colors: Primary, secondary, and accent
  • Fonts: Headings vs. body text
  • Logo: Simple and memorable

Do: Pick 2–3 main colors, 1–2 fonts, and sketch a simple logo. Consider the feelings your colors and fonts convey (fun, professional, playful).

Mini Exercise: Create a color palette and test it using free online tools like Coolors.co.
Tip: Consistency is key. Apply the same colors and fonts across all pages and design assets.
Day 5: Content Strategy

Learn: Plan what, when, and how content will be posted.

  • Content types: Text, images, video, interactive lessons
  • Frequency: Daily, weekly, monthly
  • Placement: Which pages will host which content

Do: Make a list or table of content topics for the next month. Assign each to a page.

Mini Exercise: Include short descriptions of each content piece, example: “Intro to HTML – Text + code example – Home page.”
Tip: Keep content focused on solving problems or teaching skills for your target audience.
Day 6: Goal Setting

Learn: Goals measure success. Make them SMART:

  • Specific
  • Measurable
  • Achievable
  • Relevant
  • Time-bound

Do: Write 2–3 SMART goals. Example: “50 newsletter signups in first month.”

Mini Exercise: For each goal, write how you will track it (e.g., Google Analytics, form submissions).
Tip: Align your goals with your audience needs and site purpose.
Day 7: Weekly Review

Learn: Consolidate everything from Days 1–6:

  • Purpose statement
  • Audience profiles
  • Sitemap and structure
  • Branding elements
  • Content plan
  • SMART goals

Do: Review, refine, and combine into one **comprehensive site strategy document**.

Mini Exercise: Create a checklist and mark each element complete. Ensure all sections are clear, actionable, and aligned with your purpose.
Tip: Keep this strategy handy—it will guide all design and development in the coming weeks.
